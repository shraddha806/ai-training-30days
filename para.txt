Machine Learning (ML) is a branch of AI where systems learn patterns from data to make predictions or decisions without being explicitly programmed for every rule, powering things like recommendations, fraud detection, and forecasting. 
Large Language Models (LLMs) are advanced ML models trained on massive text datasets to understand and generate human-like language, enabling chatbots, coding assistants, summarization, and content creation. 
Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) improves LLMs by connecting them to external knowledge sources such as databases or documents, so they can retrieve relevant information first and then generate more accurate, up-to-date, and context-aware responses. 
Agentic AI goes a step further by giving AI systems the ability to plan, reason, use tools, take actions, and work through multi-step goals autonomously, making them useful for complex workflows like research, automation, customer support, and business operations.
